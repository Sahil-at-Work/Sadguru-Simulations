The Law of Areas

Equal Time, Equal Area

"The line joining the planet and the Sun sweeps equal areas in equal intervals of time."

Click the trigger button below to construct equal-time orbital sweeps. Notice how the visual slices at perihelion (near focus) are narrow and long, holding the exact same area as those far away (aphelion) which are wide and short!

The Law of Periods

T² ∝ r³

"The square of its period of revolution around the Sun (T²) is directly proportional to the cube of the mean distance of a planet from the Sun (r³)."

This mathematically links planetary speed directly to how far it orbits from the parent star:

T² / r³ = Constant (K) = 1.00

Verify this absolute constraint by running planets with different radii, clicking the "Log Data" button to record their coordinates below, and looking at the resulting K ratio.
