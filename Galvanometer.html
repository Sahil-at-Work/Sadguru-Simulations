The Math Explorer

Inside the galvanometer, the deflection angle $\theta$ is reached when the magnetic torque matches the restoring spring force:

$$\theta = \left(\frac{N \cdot A \cdot B}{k}\right) \cdot I$$

$\theta$: Pointer deflection angle

$N$: Number of wire turns in loop

$B$: Magnetic flux density

$I$: Coil current input

$k$: Torsional spring stiffness (fixed)

Induction Rules

Faraday discovered that moving magnets around a loop of wire induces electrical currents because it changes the amount of magnetic flux passing through the loop:

$$\mathcal{E} = -N \frac{d\Phi}{dt}$$

The negative sign (-) is Lenz's Law, meaning the induced current will flow in a direction that opposes the original change in magnetic flux.
