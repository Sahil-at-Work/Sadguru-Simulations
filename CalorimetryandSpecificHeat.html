The Principle

The Principle of Calorimetry states that inside an insulated container (calorimeter), the heat lost by hot bodies must equal the heat gained by cold bodies.

Heat Balance Equation Q_lost = Q_gained or: ∑ Q = 0

Where heat exchanged for each component is calculated using $Q = m \cdot c \cdot \Delta T$.

Step-By-Step Solution

T_f
Step 1: Conservation of Energy
Q_s + Q_l + Q_c = 0
m_s·c_s·(T_f - T_s) + m_l·c_l·(T_f - T_l) + m_c·c_c·(T_f - T_c) = 0
Step 2: Isolate Target Variable
T_f = (m_s·c_s·T_s + m_l·c_l·T_l + m_c·c_c·T_c) / (m_s·c_s + m_l·c_l + m_c·c_c)
Step 3: Plug in Known Values
T_f = (150 · 0.385 · 100 + 200 · 4.184 · 20 + 80 · 0.9 · 20) / (150·0.385 + 200·4.184 + 80·0.9)
Step 4: Solve
T_f = 25.32 °C

Calculations assume complete thermal equilibrium with zero thermal radiation leakage to external environment.
