1. Three Classes of Physical Waves

I. Mechanical Waves Requires physical elastic matter for propagating energy (Sound waves, seismic movements, stretched strings). Cannot pass through empty vacuum space.
II. Electromagnetic Waves Oscillating electric & magnetic orthogonal vectors. Medium matter isn't needed. (Light waves, Radio signals, X-rays).
III. Matter Waves Quantum particle waves associated with moving mass molecules. (De Broglie wave dynamics).

Wave Properties Reference Matrix

Amplitude (A)

The maximum peak deviation of any particle away from its rest position. Measured in standard metric meters (m).

Double Periodicity

Waves repeat in dual domains: time and space. The distance cycle represents spatial λ, whereas temporal repeat represents T.

Wavelength (λ)

Linear spatial distance between successive particles vibrating in identical harmonic phase profiles.
