Joule's Law of Heating ($H = I^2 R t$) When an electric current flows through a conducting wire with structural resistance, drifting electrons continually collide with lattice ions. This mechanical work transfers energy, increasing atomic vibration which manifests directly as heat.
Why pick up iron without a shock? Iron is a premium electrical conductor. However, when resting neutrally on the ground, there is no potential difference ($\Delta V = 0$) applied across it. Without a driving electromotive force, no net charge carriers drift, meaning zero current flows through your hand!
The Mechanics of Overloading & Fuses Connecting multiple appliances in parallel dramatically lowers equivalent structural resistance, drawing massive current from feed lines. This extreme current causes wire temperatures to skyrocket. Safety fuses utilize low-melting alloys designed to melt away safely and terminate the circuit instantly before fires ignite.
