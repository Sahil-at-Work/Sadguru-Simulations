💡 Core Physical Insights

The speed of a transverse wave along a stretched string is determined directly by the dynamic balancing of two competing parameters: the restoring force (supplied by tension T) and the inertial property (supplied by linear mass density m = M/L).

v = √(T / m)

Newton's initial formula assumed wave propagation is isothermal ($v = \sqrt{P/\rho}$). However, Laplace corrected this, showing compression happens too rapidly for heat transfer, making it adiabatic ($v = \sqrt{\gamma P/\rho}$). Experiment with temperature and pressure below to watch the physical changes.

Newton's Formula
v = √(P / ρ) ≈ 279.9 m/s
Laplace's Corrected Formula
v = √(γP / ρ) ≈ 332.3 m/s

This is a live representation of the textbook's **Example 8.2**. Radio signals travel at the speed of light ($c = 3 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$), while physical sound waves travel at roughly $330\text{ m/s}$. Watch how physical distance changes the absolute winner of this race!
