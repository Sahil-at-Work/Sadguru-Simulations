Scale Calibration & Formulae

Establish fixed reference points: the melting point of ice (0°C / 32°F) and the boiling point of water (100°C / 212°F). Use these divisions to convert!

CONVERSION RELATIONSHIP
$$ \frac{T_F - 32}{180} = \frac{T_C}{100} $$

°C to °F: $ T_F = \frac{9}{5} T_C + 32 $

°F to °C: $ T_C = \frac{5}{9} (T_F - 32) $

Concept Lab 3

Thermometric Property Explorer

We measure temperature by tracking properties that vary linearly and monotonically. Look at how these materials react at different physical properties!

THERMOMETRIC PROPERTY CALCULATION

$$ T = \frac{P_T - P_1}{P_2 - P_1} \times 100 $$

• $ P_1 $: Property value at Ice Point (0°C)

• $ P_2 $: Property value at Steam Point (100°C)

• $ P_T $: Property value at current Temperature ($ T $)
