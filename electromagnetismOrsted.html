Scientist Profile

Hans Christian Oersted

1777 – 1851 | Denmark

In 1820, while preparing a lecture demonstration, Oersted closed an electric circuit and accidentally noticed that a nearby compass needle deviated from pointing north to pointing perpendicular to the wire.

This tiny, accidental observation permanently linked electricity and magnetism, giving birth to the study of electromagnetism and paving the way for motors, generators, and today's electronics.

🏅

In his honor, the physical unit of magnetic field strength / intensity in the CGS system is officially named the Oersted (Oe).

Physics Rulebook

Right-Hand Thumb Rule

A practical shortcut to visualize the circular direction of magnetic lines of force.

1

Imagine holding a straight current-carrying conductor in your right hand with your thumb pointing in the direction of the electric current.

2

Your curled fingers will wrap around the wire in the exact direction of the magnetic lines of force.
