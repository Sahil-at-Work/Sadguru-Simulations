Do you notice the pitch drop as a speeding train whistling past leaves you?

This auditory curiosity is the Doppler Effect, identified by Austrian physicist Christian Doppler in 1842. When a wave source and an observer move relative to each other, the observed wave frequency changes. It is a fundamental property of all wave phenomena—including sound, light, and radio!

THE MATH The Doppler Equation & Derivations

Understanding the classical formulation step-by-step.

General Formulation

When both the source and the observer (listener) are moving in the medium (with an optional wind influence), the observed frequency n is calculated from emitted frequency n₀ using:

GENERAL DOPPLER FORMULA (with wind)
n = n₀ ·
v ± v_w ± v_L
v ± v_w ∓ v_s
The Three Core Conditions
1) Source Moving & Listener Stationary (v_L = 0) v_L = 0

When source moves towards listener with $v_s$ (relative approach), wavefronts compress: n = n₀ · [v / (v - v_s)] If moving away (recede): n = n₀ · [v / (v + v_s)]

2) Listener Moving & Source Stationary (v_s = 0) v_s = 0

When listener moves towards stationary source (approaching), they cross more wavefronts per second: n = n₀ · [(v + v_L) / v] If listener is receding (away): n = n₀ · [(v - v_L) / v]

3) Both Source & Listener are Moving Both Motion

Decide signs independently! Consider each element's movement as if the other were at rest. Upper signs in numerator and denominator represent approach; lower signs represent recede.

Physics Challenge 1

The Rocket Echo Mission

A rocket speed $v_s = 220\text{ m/s}$ travels towards a stationary target. It emits a wave of frequency $n_0 = 1200\text{ Hz}$. Some sound reaches the target and gets reflected back to the rocket as an echo.

Goal: Calculate (1) frequency detected by target, and (2) frequency of echo detected by rocket! (Speed of sound = $330\text{ m/s}$).

Play with the parameters:
Interactive Velocity Diagram
1. FREQUENCY AT STATIONARY TARGET

Target acts as stationary listener. Source (Rocket) is approaching:

n = n₀ · [v / (v - v_s)]
Substitution: 1200 * [330 / (330 - 220)]
Answer: 3600 Hz
2. FREQUENCY OF ECHO AT ROCKET

Target becomes a new stationary source reflecting $n_{target}$. Rocket is listener approaching:

n' = n_target · [(v + v_L) / v]
Substitution: 3600 * [(330 + 220) / 330]
Answer: 6000 Hz

The Bat & Car Vector Lab

A bat flying at velocity $V_B$ is followed by a car driving at velocity $V_C$. But they aren't on the same line! They have offset directions.

🔑 Core Rule: If velocity vectors are not on the same straight line, we must project their components along the immediate line joining them to evaluate Doppler shift!

Adjust Speeds & Angles:
Car Speed ($v_c$) 50 m/s
Car Offset Angle (θ_c) 53°
Bat Speed ($v_B$) 12.5 m/s
Bat Offset Angle (θ_B) 37°
Interactive Vector Projection
Dynamic Vector Projections:
🚙 Car Component along line of sight: V_c_line = V_c · cos(θ_c)
50 · cos(53°) ≈ 30.00 m/s
🦇 Bat Component along line of sight: V_B_line = V_B · cos(θ_B)
12.5 · cos(37°) ≈ 10.00 m/s
🚨 Step 1: Bat transmits ultrasonic sound (n₀ = 36 kHz) to Car

Source (Bat) is receding (denominator gets $+$). Listener (Car) is approaching (numerator gets $+$):

n = 36000 * [(350 + 30) / (350 + 10)] = 38000 Hz

Sound vs. Light: Key Differences

Who is in motion?

In Sound, it is absolutely crucial to distinguish whether the source or listener is moving, because wave speed depends strictly on the physical medium. In Light, because its speed is absolute ($c$), only the relative velocity between both particles matters.

Wind Effects

Wind shifts the entire medium carrying sound waves. Hence, wind velocity ($v_w$) must be added or subtracted to the velocity of sound waves. Light waves propagate through empty vacuum; no material wind medium exists to alter its velocity.

Relativity Shifts

Classical Doppler formulations perfectly model sound waves. However, for high-speed light waves (approaching $c$), Einstein's relativistic effects (time dilation) must be applied, resulting in different math for Light!
