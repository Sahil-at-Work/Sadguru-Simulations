Convection Dynamics

Under Natural Convection, water molecules adjacent to the heating boundary expand, density decreases ($\rho \downarrow$), and buoyancy forces push them upward. Cool, dense molecules descend along the beaker perimeter, setting up circular Convection Currents.

Under Forced Convection, a mechanical propeller forces fluid circulation, augmenting convective heat transfer rates beyond simple gravity-driven limits.
